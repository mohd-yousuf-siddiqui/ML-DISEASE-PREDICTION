About Diabetes Prediction

This diabetes prediction tool uses a machine learning model trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset, which is widely used in machine learning for diabetes prediction.

Understanding the Parameters:

  • Pregnancies: Number of times pregnant - can affect insulin resistance.
  • Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration in an oral glucose tolerance test - high levels can indicate diabetes.
  • Blood Pressure: Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg) - hypertension is often comorbid with diabetes.
  • Skin Thickness: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm) - a measure of body fat.
  • Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml) - abnormal levels can indicate insulin resistance or deficiency.
  • BMI: Body Mass Index - weight in kg/(height in m)² - obesity increases risk of type 2 diabetes.
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function: A function that scores likelihood of diabetes based on family history.
  • Age: Age in years - risk increases with age.

Diabetes Risk Factors:

Type 1 Diabetes:
  • Family history of diabetes
  • Certain genetic factors
  • Environmental triggers
Type 2 Diabetes:
  • Overweight or obesity
  • Physical inactivity
  • Family history of diabetes
  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • High blood pressure
  • Abnormal cholesterol levels
  • History of gestational diabetes
Important Note

This prediction is based on statistical models and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult with a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.
